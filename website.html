The Lazy Farmer

here was once a lazy farmer named Tom who always looked for shortcuts. One day, he decided to plant corn but didn’t want to put in the effort to water the seeds. Instead, he hoped for rain. Days passed, and the seeds didn’t sprout. Tom’s neighbor, a hardworking farmer, noticed this and asked, “Why aren’t you watering your crops?” Tom replied, “I’m waiting for the rain to do the work for me.” The neighbor laughed and said, “Tom, if you want a good harvest, you have to put in the effort. Rain or no rain, you need to water your crops.” Realizing his mistake, Tom started watering his seeds daily. Soon, his corn grew tall and healthy, and he had a bountiful harvest.

The Procrastinating Student

A Short Story

There was a student named Sam who always procrastinated. One day, he had a big exam but decided to watch TV instead of studying. The night before the exam, he realized he hadn’t studied at all. Panicking, Sam grabbed his books and started cramming. He stayed up all night, trying to learn everything in a few hours. The next day, he was exhausted but went to take the exam anyway. To his surprise, the questions were all about topics he had crammed the night before. Sam managed to pass the exam, but just barely. He realized that while he got lucky this time, he couldn’t rely on last-minute cramming for future success.

The Forgetful Chef

A Short Story

There was a chef named Lisa who was known for her delicious dishes but also for being very forgetful. One day, she was preparing a big meal for a famous food critic. In her rush, she accidentally used salt instead of sugar in her dessert recipe. When she realized her mistake, it was too late to start over. Thinking quickly, Lisa decided to turn her mistake into a new creation. She added some savory ingredients and turned the dessert into a unique sweet-and-salty treat. The food critic loved it and praised Lisa for her creativity and bold flavors. Her “mistake” became a hit, and she added it to her menu as a signature dish.
